So what are 3D Printing and 3D Scanning?

3D Printing is a technology that creates three-dimensional objects by building up layers of material based on a digital model.
This enables rapid and customized manufacturing in various industries, including medicine, automotive, and design.

3D Scanning is used to digitize physical objects by creating an accurate digital representation. It is often used in reverse engineering,
medical research, art conservation, and industrial applications.


How does the technology work?

  • FDM (Fused Deposition Modeling):

    Melts and extrudes plastic filament layer by layer.

Example of FDM Printer then regular PLA FDM Plastic filament to a finished product. In this picture a locomotive


  • SLA (Stereolithography):

    Hardens liquid resin with a laser.

A common affordable SLA printer, where liquid material is used and finally a finished detailed product.


  • SLS (Selective Laser Sintering):

    Uses a laser to melt powder material and create detailed objects.

SLS Printers are most common for companies. The material for this machine is powder and yes, you can see the result yourself.


Applications

3D technology is used in a number of areas:

  • Medicine:

    Prosthetics, implants and anatomical models.

  • Industry:

    Prototypes and custom tools.

  • Art and design:

    Sculptures, fashion and custom-designed products.

  • Architecture:

    Creating models for building projects.
